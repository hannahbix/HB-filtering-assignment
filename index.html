Health Care Coverage and Immigration Status

By Hannah Bottum

March 22, 2022

Last week, legislation to expand proposed legislation backed by immigrant rights groups that would expand health care options for undocumented immigants passed a major milestone. The New York State Senate and Assembly allocated $345 million in their proposed budgets for the program expansion. State lawmakers will meet with New York Governor Kathy Hochul in the coming weeks to negotiate the final budget.

The current administration under President Joe Biden has also announced plans to rewrite the "public charge" rule at the federal level. Under former President Donald Trump, the expansion of the "public charge" rule enabled immigration officers to take current or potential future need for public services like Medicaid into account in immigration cases.
